//Diagramming Urban Infrastructure, Automative Experience, and Steering Experiential Systems//

// about our tour offerings

ITITIT inc. dozents invite visitors to join them for a guided tour of East Valley urban infrastructure. We will traverse the city the way most Phoenicians do: from the seat of a car. On this tour, however, we’ll also be encounter Tempe and West Mesa’s in a different way: amplifying electromagnetic field using the car’s AM radio.

A sample of a nighttime ride..

When tuned to 530kHz, the AM radio can detect fluctuations in the electromagnetic field caused by power lines and other features of bundles of electricity coursing through the city. As we drive through the city, we leverage the electrical and roadway architectures of the grid city par excellence as a playable environment. Unseen fields are made audible. Analog augmented reality.

Marshall McLuhan wrote that technologies function as extensions of man, and that electrical media in particular extended man’s nervous systems. When a musical signal briefly penetrates the sonic space, we’re reminded that we - we humans - are meant to be assumed all causation for these sounds and these signals. Indeed the (an)archival aspect of this project is to register the hum of the city, the machine at work.

But what’s largely extensive in this electromagnetic space is radically non-human. Afterall the electromagnetic field and electricity itself doesn’t particularly “care” about humans anyway. Important to note, that this is obviously not a Situationist “drift” or derive. Nor is it sound walk on wheels, nor rowboat phenomenology.

It is an experiential and steered complex system. Of course we are not steering not steering the city as one might a complex simulation or Sim City , but the level of the observer. inherently affects the state of the higher system.
